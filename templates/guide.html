How to Use Your 4-in-1 Soil Testing Sensor

Simple step-by-step guide for farmers

What This Tool Does

This sensor helps you know your soil health by checking 4 important things: pH level (acidity), Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K). This information helps you grow better crops and save money on fertilizers.

📊 pH Level

Checks if soil is acidic or alkaline

🌿 Nitrogen (N)

Helps plants grow green leaves

🌾 Phosphorus (P)

Helps roots and flowers grow

🌻 Potassium (K)

Makes plants strong and healthy

1

Unbox and Check All Parts

Open the box carefully and make sure you have:

  • Main sensor unit with display screen
  • Long probe with 4 metal rods
  • Connection cable
  • User manual
💡 Tip: Keep all parts in a dry and safe place when not using.
2

Connect the Probe to the Device

Take the probe (the part with 4 metal rods) and connect it to the main device using the cable. Make sure the connection is tight and secure. You will hear a small click sound when it's properly connected.

⚠️ Important: Do not force the connection. If it doesn't fit easily, check if you're connecting it the right way.
3

Give Power to the Device

Your device can get power in different ways. Check which type you have:

  • Battery Powered: Insert batteries (usually 2-4 AA batteries) in the battery compartment. Open the back cover, put batteries matching the + and - signs, then close the cover.
  • Rechargeable Battery: Use the USB charging cable. Connect one end to the device and other end to a phone charger, power bank, or computer. Red light means charging, green light means fully charged. First charge takes 3-4 hours.
  • Direct Power: Some models work with adapters. Plug the adapter into electricity socket and connect to the device.
⚠️ Important: Don't mix old and new batteries. Remove batteries if not using device for long time. Don't overcharge rechargeable devices (unplug after green light).
4

Turn On the Device

Press and hold the power button (usually marked as ON/OFF or ⏻) for 2-3 seconds. The screen will light up and show "Ready" or display numbers like "0.0". You may hear a small beep sound.

If the device doesn't turn on:

  • Check if batteries are inserted correctly
  • Check if rechargeable battery is charged
  • Try replacing batteries with new ones
  • Make sure power adapter is properly connected
💡 Tip: Always keep spare batteries with you when going to the field. Charge the device night before testing.
5

Choose Your Testing Spot

Walk to your farm field and choose a good spot to test:

  • Pick a spot in the middle of your field (not near edges)
  • Avoid areas near trees, roads, or water drainage
  • Remove any grass, stones, or plant remains from the surface
  • The soil should be slightly moist (not too dry or too wet)
💡 Tip: Test 3-5 different spots in your field and take the average reading for best results.
6

Insert the Probe into Soil

Push the probe straight down into the soil. Make sure:

  • All 4 metal rods go completely into the soil
  • Push it at least 4-6 inches (10-15 cm) deep
  • The probe should be straight, not tilted
  • Press firmly but don't force it if soil is too hard
⚠️ Important: If soil is too hard, water it lightly and wait 10-15 minutes before testing. Don't bend the metal rods.
7

Wait for Readings

After inserting the probe, wait for 30-60 seconds. The device screen will show numbers for each measurement. Don't move the probe while it's reading.

The screen will display:

  • pH: A number between 3-10
  • N: Nitrogen level in mg/kg
  • P: Phosphorus level in mg/kg
  • K: Potassium level in mg/kg
💡 Tip: Write down the readings on paper or take a photo of the screen to remember later.
8

Remove the Probe Carefully

After getting the reading, pull the probe straight up and out of the soil. Clean the metal rods with a soft cloth or tissue to remove soil. Don't leave soil on the rods.

⚠️ Important: Don't wash the probe with water directly. Just wipe it clean with a dry or slightly damp cloth.
9

Understand Your Readings

Check the table below to understand what your readings mean:

📖 Reading Guide for Your Soil

pH Levels

pH Value Soil Type What to Do
3.0 - 5.5 Too Acidic Add lime powder to increase pH
5.5 - 7.5 Good for Most Crops Perfect! No action needed
7.5 - 10.0 Too Alkaline Add sulfur or organic compost

Nitrogen (N) Levels

N Value (mg/kg) Level What to Do
0 - 30 Low Add urea or cow dung manure
30 - 60 Medium Add small amount of nitrogen fertilizer
60 - 100+ Good No need to add nitrogen

Phosphorus (P) Levels

P Value (mg/kg) Level What to Do
0 - 10 Low Add DAP or bone meal fertilizer
10 - 25 Medium Add some phosphorus fertilizer
25 - 50+ Good No need to add phosphorus

Potassium (K) Levels

K Value (mg/kg) Level What to Do
0 - 40 Low Add potash or wood ash
40 - 80 Medium Add some potassium fertilizer
80 - 150+ Good No need to add potassium

🔧 How to Take Care of Your Sensor

  • Clean the metal probe after every use with a dry cloth
  • Store the device in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Don't drop the device or put heavy things on it
  • Charge the battery regularly, don't let it fully drain
  • Keep the probe away from water when not testing
  • If device shows wrong readings, clean the probe with alcohol and dry cloth
  • Test the device every 2-3 months to make sure it works properly

🌟 Final Tips for Best Results

  • Test your soil at the same time of day for consistent results
  • Best time to test is early morning or late afternoon
  • Test your soil before planting and after harvest
  • Keep a notebook to track readings over time
  • If readings seem wrong, test again in a different spot
  • Don't test right after heavy rain or watering
  • Share results with your agricultural officer for advice
